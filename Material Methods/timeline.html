    19th Century

    The rise of mass production began in stores, with patriarchs like Arnold Constable & Company and A.T. Stewart Dry Goods Store in the 1800s. As men have dominated production in this country, women have dictated the adornment of fabric goods through their corporeal volunteerism, sometimes by enforcement, as models, or as ascribed characters in advertisements. The magazine launched as the first concept of marketing advertisements, in the mid to late 1800s, as a monthly periodical, founded and edited by Isabella Mary Beeton and her husband, Samuel Orchart Beeton. Because of its affordability, the magazine reached a larger audience of younger, middle-class women, rather than the English bourgeoisie. This platform served not only as a fashion news outlet but also as a politically safe space for women to discuss their struggles with gender roles and domesticity.
